A little bit of info about who I am and what my interests are.


Education


I am in my second year of the Mechatronics Engineering Co-op Program at McMaster University. I have taken a variety of courses such as engineering mechanics, programming, electrodynamics and thermodynamics. I have also worked on projects with practical engineering application, including a mechanical lever device for a client with rheumatoid arthritis and a 3D printed CD-ROM reader gearbox.


Programming


Over the course of my studies and personal projects, I have tried to learn and use a variety of programming languages, tools and frameworks depending on the task at hand. In classroom environments I have extensivly used Python, Java and Matlab languages. In my spare time I am also learning the fundamentals of web design, including HTML, CSS, Javascript and frameworks like Bootstrap.


Hardware


Recently I have started working with microcontrollers such as the Ardunio board as a fun way to get introduced to the world of computer hardware and embedded systems. I am very interested in the field of robotics and am using the Ardunio architecture to build personal projects such as a Sumobot for a school competetion and the Teachbot system to build a complete automated system.


Music


Of course not all of my time is spent doing Mechatronics related projects. Another one of my passions is playing and creating music. I have been a guitar player for about 7 years, and have also started teaching for the Guitar Club at McMaster. I mostly play and listen to rock genres, and artists such as Black Sabbath, Led Zeppelin, Pink Floyd and the Arkells.


Work Expereince


Throughout highschool I was employed at McDonalds, where I was later promoted to crew trainer and acquired skills in leading, training and dealing with customers on a regular basis. I finished my first co-op at Nahanni Steel where I operated a variety of manufacturing machines and steel presses. I recieved an Outstanding rating and produced a record 2381 parts in a single shift.
